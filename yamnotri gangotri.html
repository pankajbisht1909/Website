Overview

Embark on a spiritual journey to the Do Dham of Yamunotri and Gangotri, two of the holiest sites in Hinduism, nestled in the pristine Himalayas. Start your pilgrimage at Yamunotri, where the sacred Yamuna River originates from the Champasar Glacier. The temple dedicated to Goddess Yamuna is a serene place of worship surrounded by majestic mountains and lush greenery. From Yamunotri, travel to Gangotri, the origin of the holy Ganges River. The Gangotri temple, dedicated to Goddess Ganga, is a place of immense spiritual significance and offers stunning views of the surrounding peaks. Take a dip in the icy waters of the Ganges, believed to cleanse the soul of sins. The journey between Yamunotri and Gangotri is filled with breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains, pristine rivers, and lush forests. The Yamunotri Gangotri tour is not just a pilgrimage but also an opportunity to connect with nature and experience the tranquility of the Himalayas. The entire journey is a test of endurance and faith, with devotees braving challenging terrain and weather conditions to pay homage to the divine. Whether you're a devout pilgrim or a nature enthusiast, the Yamunotri Gangotri tour promises an unforgettable experience that will stay with you long after you've returned home.

Itinerary

Day 01: Pickup in Haridwar
Meet our driver at Delhi Airport/Railway Station for a scenic drive to Haridwar. Check into the hotel and witness the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri. Overnight stay in Haridwar.
Day 02: Haridwar to Barkot (200km, 6 hours)
After breakfast, drive to Barkot with a visit to the picturesque Kempty Fall in Mussoorie. Enjoy the scenic drive through the Jaunsar Mountains and admire the old architecture along the banks of the River Yamuna. Check into the hotel in Barkot for an overnight stay.
Day 03: Barkot -Yamunotri- Barkot (40km by road, 6km trek each way)
Begin the day with a drive to Jankichatti, followed by a trek or a horse/Doli ride to the sacred Yamunotri Temple. Take a dip in the rejuvenating waters of the Surya Kund and offer prayers to Goddess Yamuna. Cook packed rice in the natural hot springs as Prashad. Return to Jankichatti and drive back to Barkot for dinner and overnight stay.
Day 04: Barkot to Uttarkashi (90km, 4 hours)
After breakfast, drive to Uttarkashi with a visit to the Shiv Cave along the way. Check into the hotel and explore the Kashi Vishwanath Temple and local market in the evening. Dinner and overnight stay in Uttarkashi.
Day 05: Uttarkashi - Gangotri -Uttarkashi (100km, 4 hours each way)
Embark on a day trip to Gangotri, stopping at Gangnani to take a dip in the hot springs. Visit the Gangotri Temple and soak in the breathtaking views of the Harshil Valley. Return to Uttarkashi for dinner and overnight stay.
Day 06: Uttarkashi to Haridwar (190km, 7 hours)
Early morning drive to Haridwar, passing by the scenic views of Mussoorie and Dehradun. Check into the hotel in Haridwar and relax. Overnight stay in Haridwar.
Day 07: Departure from Haridwar to Delhi (220km, 6 hours)
After breakfast, drive back Airport/Railway Station for your onward journey. .
