Overview

The Char Dham Yatra is a revered pilgrimage circuit in the Indian Himalayas, encompassing four sacred sites: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. Each destination holds profound religious significance for Hindus, representing the source of the Yamuna, Ganges, Mandakini, and Alaknanda rivers, respectively. Pilgrims undertake this arduous journey seeking spiritual cleansing and blessings. The yatra is not just a physical journey but also a deeply spiritual experience, offering a chance to connect with one's inner self and with the divine. The rugged terrain and high altitudes add to the challenge, making it a test of both physical endurance and faith. Despite the hardships, millions of devotees undertake this yatra every year, believing that the journey purifies their souls and brings them closer to God.
The Char Dham Yatra is not just a pilgrimage; it's a manifestation of unwavering faith and devotion that has been part of Indian culture for centuries. The Char Dham Yatra is steeped in mythology, with each destination associated with legends and tales from ancient Hindu scriptures. The journey is believed to wash away sins and lead to moksha (liberation) from the cycle of birth and death. Itinerary

Day 01: Haridwar to Barkot (200km, 6hr)
Meet at Haridwar Railway Station/Dehradun Airport, drive to Barkot. En route, visit Kempty Fall in Mussoorie. Check into the hotel in Barkot for overnight stay.
Day 02: Barkot - Yamunotri - Barkot (40km, 6km trek each side)
After breakfast, drive to Jankichatti and trek/hire horse/Doli to Yamunotri Temple. Take a dip in Surya Kund and visit the temple. Cook packed rice in hot spring water as Prashad. Return to Jankichatti, drive to Barkot, and stay overnight.
Day 03: Barkot to Uttarkashi (90km, 4hr)
Drive to Uttarkashi, visit Shiv Cave en route. Check into the hotel and visit Kashi Vishwanath Temple and local market in the evening. Overnight stay in Uttarkashi.
Day 04: Uttarkashi - Gangotri - Uttarkashi (100km, 4hr each side)
Drive to Gangotri, stop at Gangnani for a dip in hot spring water. Visit Gangotri Temple and Harshil Valley. Return to Uttarkashi for overnight stay.
Day 05: Uttarkashi to Guptkashi (200km, 8hr)
Drive to Guptakshi, check into the hotel, and stay overnight.
Day 06: Guptkashi - Gaurikund - Kedarnath (25km drive, 19km trek)
Drive to Gaurikund and trek/hire Doli/horse to Kedarnath Temple. Check into the hotel in Kedarnath, visit the temple, and witness the evening Aarti. Dinner and overnight stay in Kedarnath. Day 07: Kedarnath - Gaurikund - Guptkashi (25km drive, 19km trek)
Morning visit to Kedarnath Temple, then return to Guptkashi. Check into the hotel and stay overnight. Day 08: Guptkashi - Badrinath (190km, 8hr)
Drive to Badrinath, check into the hotel, and visit Badrinath Temple. Overnight stay in Badrinath.
Day 09: Badrinath - Joshimath - Rudrapriyag (160km, 6hr)
Visit Mana Village, Vyas Cave, Bhim Bridge, and Saraswati River. Drive to Rudrapriyag, visit Narsingh Devta Temple in Joshimath. Check into the hotel in Rudrapriyag for overnight stay.
Day 10: Rudrapriyag to Haridwar (160km, 6hr)
Drive to Haridwar, visit Dhari Devi Temple, Devpriyag, and Rishikesh en route. Drop at Haridwar Railway Station or nearby. .
