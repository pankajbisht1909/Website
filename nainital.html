TRAVEL FAR ENOUGH,TO MEET YOURSELF

About

Nainital is located in the Kumaon foothills of the outer Himalayas at a distance of 276 km (171 mi) from the state capital Dehradun and 314 km (195 mi) from New Delhi, the capital of India. Situated at an altitude of 1,938 metres (6,358 ft) above sea level, the town is set in a valley containing an eye-shaped lake, approximately two miles in circumference, and surrounded by mountains, of which the highest are Naina Peak (2,615 m (8,579 ft)) on the north, Deopatha (2,438 m (7,999 ft)) on the west, and Ayarpatha (2,278 m (7,474 ft)) on the south. From the tops of the higher peaks, "magnificent views can be obtained of the vast plain to the south, or of the mass of tangled ridges lying north, bound by the great snowy range which forms the central axis of the Himalayas." The hill station attracts tourists round the year.

PLACES

TO VISIT WITHIN

NANDA DEVI

The Naina Devi temple is considered as one of the most sacred temples in the country showering blessings on devotees from across the world. The great Shivalik range of Himalayas surround the town and the temple is guarded by the highest peak Mt. Nanda Devi which is believed to be the sister of goddess Naina Devi.

MALL ROAD

Mall Road in Nainital is a charming stretch lined with shops, restaurants, and hotels, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills and the Naini Lake. It's a bustling hub for tourists and locals alike, perfect for leisurely strolls and shopping for local handicrafts

SNOW VIEW

Snow View in Nainital is a popular vantage point offering panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayas, including Nanda Devi, Trisul, and Nanda Kot peaks. Accessible by a cable car or a short trek, it's a must-visit for breathtaking mountain vistas and a serene escape from the bustling town below.

ECO CAVE GARDEN

Eco Cave Garden in Nainital is a natural park featuring interconnected caves and hanging gardens, offering visitors a glimpse into the region's ecological diversity. The caves, modeled after different animal habitats, provide an educational and adventurous experience, making it a fascinating destination for nature lovers and families alike.

NAINA PEAK

Naina Peak, also known as China Peak, is the highest point in Nainital. Situated at an altitude of 2,615 meters (8,579 feet), it offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan ranges and the town below. A popular trekking destination, Naina Peak is a must-visit for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, providing a peaceful retreat amidst stunning natural beauty.

HOW TO REACHS

BY PLANE

The closest airport to Nainital is the Pantnagar Airport in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand. It is situated approximately two hours away from the city centre. Located at a distance of 70 kilometres, direct flights to Pantnagar are available only from New Delhi.
BY TRAIN

The nearest railhead to Nainital is the Kathgodam Railway station, situated 34 kilometres from the main city centre. It is located in Haldwani in the Nainital district of the Uttarakhand State of India. This railway station also enjoys good connectivity with cities such as New Delhi, Lucknow and prominent places such as Howrah.
BY ROAD

If you are looking for a road trip and wish to enjoy the journey as much as the destination; Nainital is the perfect travel destination for you. It is well linked to all the major cities and towns around it, especially with National Highway 87 that connects it to Rampur and Rudrapur. You are in for a treat if you are travelling from Delhi, with Nainital being only a six to seven-hour drive from the capital along with a few halts en route.
